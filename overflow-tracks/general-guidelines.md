## Award Structure

Prizes for Sui Overflow 2026 will follow a **split distribution model** designed to encourage continued development beyond the hackathon:

- **50% of the prize** will be awarded upon announcement of winners
- **50% of the prize** will be awarded after successful mainnet deployment

If a winning team has already deployed their project to mainnet by the time winners are announced in August, they will receive **100% of the prize upfront**.

## Notes

- Mainnet deployment must meet the minimum functional requirements as defined by the Sui team and/or track sponsors.
- Teams are encouraged to continue building post-hackathon to unlock the full prize amount.
- Additional support, opportunities, and ecosystem pathways may be available for top-performing teams.

# Demo Day Requirements

Projects that are shortlisted will advance to Demo Day and must meet the following requirements:

### Attendance

At least one team member must be present during your assigned Demo Day session.

### Demo Day Schedule

**July 20, 2026**

- Agentic Web Track
- Walrus Track

**July 21, 2026**

- DeFi & Payments Track
- DeepBook Track

****Detailed schedule will be shared once the shortlisted teams have been announced.***

### Presentation Requirements

Shortlisted teams will be required to:

- Present their project live to judges
- Demonstrate a working version of their project
- Answer questions from judges during the Q&A portion

### Demo Day Format

- **Presentation Time:** 5 minutes per team
- **Q&A:** Up to 2 additional minutes, if time allows
- Timing is strictly enforced

Please ensure your presentation is concise and focused on:

- The problem you are solving
- Your solution
- Technical implementation
- Why you built on Sui
- Future roadmap

### Absence Policy

If a team is absent during their assigned presentation slot, they will be called again at the end of the session.

Failure to appear after a second call may result in forfeiture from the competition.

### Meeting Link & Calendar Invite

- Zoom meeting links will be shared directly via email.
- A calendar invitation will also be sent to shortlisted teams.
- All participants are encouraged to join a few minutes before their scheduled presentation time.

### Livestream

Demo Day sessions will also be streamed live on YouTube for the broader community.

❓ FAQ for Project Submission

- **Should each team member submit the project individually, or should one person submit for the entire team?**

    The main POC should be creating the project, and in the projects you can add your teammembers.

- When should we create/submit a project on DeepSurge?

    Earlier the better. You can edit your submissions at anytime post creation of your project; you cannot submit a new project post deadline.

- **Can we submit the same project to multiple tracks or categories?**

    You may only choose ONE track per project. However, if you have a different project, you may submit a different project under a different or same track.

- **Can I keep my GitHub repository private?**

    During judging period, we require your repository to be public.

- **Can I join multiple teams or submit multiple projects?**

    Yes! You are welcome to try multiple projects and multiple teams. Just make sure your team leader, who will submit your project, updates you into the team for registration.

- **Do we need to submit a video demo or presentation along with the project?**

    Yes, a video demo is a must. The video should not be more than 5 minutes long, and content after 5 minutes will not be considered.

- **Can I continue working on the project after the submission deadline?**

    Yes! This is highly encouraged. We have follow-up hackathons + demodays resulting post-Overflow, and you will get a head start!

- **What are the criteria for judging submissions?**

    The exact judging process will be shared soon, but it will include a combination of scores from individual judges and votes from the community.

- **Do we need to deploy the project to Mainnet?**

    The project either needs to be deployed on testnet or mainnet.

- **What if a team member drops out?**

    POC, team leader should update their project details to avoid confusion and conflict.

</aside>
