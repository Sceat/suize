# SUIZE — "TRANSMISSION" — Veo 3.1 Prompts

A ~44-second viral intro for Suize (the agent-discovery layer for Sui — agents ask in plain English, get structured answers in 240ms, pay per call in gasless USDsui via x402). A single continuous voiceover delivers the entire product explanation as prophecy while the visuals descend into agentic-AI psychedelia filtered through 1968 recovered-archival footage — as if a BBC documentary crew accidentally intercepted a machine intelligence dreaming about itself.

The voiceover is the spine. Every visual is wallpaper for the voice. **Each shot prompt below already embeds its specific voiceover segment** — Veo 3.1 will speak the line natively during the gen (no visible speaker required, the voice is narration over the visuals). The master voiceover script section below is kept as the reference arc and as a fallback for ElevenLabs re-recording if Veo's native voice doesn't land.

## Visual Style Bible

Every shot uses the same dual lens — read this once, it applies to all eight prompts below.

- **Capture medium**: 1968 BBC archival broadcast, recovered 16mm black and white film, heavy grain, gate weave, faint vertical scratch lines drifting down the frame, optical-track audio compression on any incidental sound, faint warm projector hum baseline under everything.
- **Color discipline**: monochrome dominant, but a single saturated **cyan** bleed leaks through wherever agentic intelligence is present (eyes, slits, tendrils, packets) — the only color that survives the archival treatment.
- **Content**: every shot depicts **agentic AI psychedelia** — machine intelligence experiencing itself. Think Deep Dream meets Refik Anadol meets Tarkovsky's Stalker meets Annihilation's shimmer. Hexagonal grids that breathe, fractals that organize, neural constellations forming and dissolving, liquid metal that thinks.
- **No dialogue in any shot.** The voiceover lives on a separate track. Each Veo gen should produce ambient atmosphere only — drones, hums, distant electrical pulses.

## Production Order

1. **Generate each shot with its embedded voiceover** in Veo 3.1. Shots 1–7 each contain their VO segment in the audio direction — Veo will speak the line natively. Re-roll any shot where the voice pronunciation, pacing, or accent drifts from the intended sermon delivery.
2. **Atmosphere visuals** (the agentic-psychedelic shots): Shots 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7. Generate each at 6-second duration except Shot 7 at 5 seconds. Re-roll until the cyan-only color discipline holds across all of them — Veo sometimes leaks other colors into archival B&W; reject those takes.
3. **Mechanism shots** (Shots 5 and 6) need to feel like the same continuous broadcast — generate them back-to-back in one Veo session for visual and voice consistency.
4. **Shot 8 — the sigil** is the only non-psychedelic shot. Three seconds, candle-lit chalkboard, no voiceover, only the bell.
5. **Voice consistency pass**: listen to all seven VO segments back-to-back. If the voice pitch, accent, or timbre drifts between gens, regenerate the outliers until they match — OR fall back to re-recording the entire continuous script in ElevenLabs v3 using the master script above and the voice generation settings, then mute Veo's native audio and lay the ElevenLabs take over the full edit as one track.
6. **Suno score** generated last, cut to voiceover length.
7. **Final assembly**: hard cuts between shots, voiceover continuous across cuts (Veo's native or ElevenLabs replacement), Suno score mixed low underneath. The sermon binds the eight psychedelic visuals into one transmission.

The viewer should finish the 44 seconds understanding three things they could not have explained at second zero: **agents need to ask, Sui is where they can read, Suize is where they can ask.** Everything else is wallpaper for that arrival.
