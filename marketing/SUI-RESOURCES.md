### The amplification LAW digest (violate any → not amplified)
1. **Capitalization:** "Sui" = the network (never "SUI", never "Sui Network" with capital N); "SUI"/"$SUI" = token only. Same pattern for Walrus/WAL.
2. **Never imply partnership** without a documented one: ✅ "built on Sui" / "live on @SuiNetwork" — ❌ "we partnered with @SuiNetwork".
3. **No token/financial talk:** no prices, yields, APR/APY, trading language, memecoins. Lead with utility. (Our 2% fee talk is fine — it's product pricing, not token talk.)
4. **No hype:** no 🚀🔥💰-class emojis, no superlatives ("best/#1/only"), no future-casting, no "we're live!" with zero substance. Their test: *"does this serve me, or the Sui community?"*
5. **Funding posts:** never highlight a Foundation dollar amount; focus on what the product enables.
6. **Decentralization language:** Sui isn't owned by anyone; Mysten = "original contributor"; keep Foundation/Mysten/Walrus Foundation distinct.
7. New-to-ecosystem teams can be disqualified, **except "projects that provide a public good to the network"** — frame the open rail + OSS middleware + the x402-Sui scheme as exactly that.
8. Educational > promotional. The tweet that gets RT'd explains *how agent payments work on Sui*, not that Suize exists.

  "Everything below is live on Sui testnet today and verifiable on-chain: (1) `account.move` — a four-verb USDC payment rail (send / charge / subscription-charge / open pay) with on-chain subscriptions (fixed payee + per-period cap + Clock), a shared fee-policy object, and 100 passing Move tests; (2) a stateless payment facilitator (build → agent signs locally → submit → verify) with Enoki-sponsored gas and an ~60-line HTTP-402 merchant middleware, proven by 18/18 end-to-end tests on testnet; (3) Suize Deploy — agents pay to deploy websites to Walrus; it shipped our own production landing page, served via a Cloudflare worker that hash-verifies every file against the on-chain manifest; (4) a consumer wallet with Google zkLogin onboarding and gasless flows; (5) a DeepBook Predict trading demo (Crash). - **Technical architecture:**
  "One on-chain rail, many consumers. A shared `Account<USDC>` object holds an agent's spendable balance — the deposit IS the hard cap, enforced by the chain, with one-tap withdraw/kill. Four verbs: free P2P `spend`; `charge` (one-off merchant charge); `charge_subscription` (permissionless renewals leashed by on-chain terms: fixed payee, per-period cap, Clock); and open `pay` (any address pays any address — no Suize account needed on either side). Fee policy lives in a Suize-owned shared `RailConfig` (default 2%, per-merchant overrides, admin-cap gated); every merchant payment emits a receipt event with the fee visible, so volume is auditable and wash-resistant by construction. Off-chain: a deterministic, stateless facilitator (no AI in the money path) builds and sponsors transactions via Enoki — the payer always signs with its own key (zkLogin session or a Sui-native agent's wallet); merchants integrate via a ~60-line HTTP-402 middleware or a zero-code pay-link; verification is a single transaction read — the chain is the database. Walrus stores deployed sites and the wallet's verifiable agent action log."
- **Business model & fee structure:**
  "2% on merchant payments with no fixed floor (vs. ~30¢ floors on cards and 4% on agentic checkout incumbents), taken inline at settlement and emitted in the on-chain receipt; P2P sends are free. Per-merchant override rates enable founding-merchant 0% intro periods. Second line: Suize Deploy — $0.50 per agent-paid deploy plus a subscription for custom domains and auto-renewing Walrus storage. Third line: premium AI tier subscription in the consumer wallet. Moonshots growth incentives would be distributed through the rail itself: funded starter sub-accounts, dual-sided referral rewards released only after a real funded payment, and founding-merchant fee subsidies — every incentive lands as verifiable on-chain activity."
